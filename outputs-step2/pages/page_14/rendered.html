To implement Step 2i), i.e, to construct the objective function (32) , we produce a random draw ω = ( y , w , ϵ 1 , ϵ 2 ) , use the decision rules for value function V ( y , w ; θ) , consumption share c w = ϕ ( y , w ; θ) and Lagrange multiplier h = h ( y , w ; θ) and transition equations (21) and (23) .

The choice of weights v and v h is a nontrivial problem. While in the previous methods, we expressed the residuals in unit free form, so that we could use unit weights, it is not a feasible approach here. Value function for our model has the range from negative to positive numbers. If we do it unit free, we will get sometimes a division by numbers close to zero. Additionally, the residuals can switch from positive to negative leading to spurious iterations of the gradient algorithm. In our application, we choose weights so that the size of the residuals was approximately the same in all three equations.
